### 📘 Assignment 1: Comparative Analysis of SDLC Methodologies

* **Focus Areas**: Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) phases, Traditional Waterfall Model vs. Agile Methodology, organizational culture, and workflow efficiency.
* **Reflective Insight**: 
  * Crafting this comparative analysis forced me to challenge the historical hegemony of the sequential Waterfall approach. I discovered that while Waterfall excels in structural documentation and guarantees fixed deliverables under immutable boundaries, its rigid phases make it practically obsolete in today's fast-paced tech workspace.
  * Researching Agile's adaptive frameworks (e.g., Scrum iterations lasting 1–2 months) opened my eyes to how dynamic requirement adjustments maximize client satisfaction. I realized that as engineers, we must not view incoming project scope revisions as continuous setbacks, but rather welcome them as incremental opportunities to polish system architecture.

### 📊 Assignment 2: Project Management and Economic Feasibility

* **Focus Areas**: Feasibility Studies (Operational, Technical, Economic), Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA), Net Present Value (NPV), Profitability Index (PI), Work Breakdown Structures (WBS), and PERT Critical Path Method (CPM).
* **Reflective Insight**: 
  * This assignment bridged the gap between pure technical system modeling and corporate financial strategy. Conducting the economic feasibility study for the *SkorBestari* project was highly eye-opening; it proved that software design cannot exist in a vacuum separated from fiscal constraints. 
  * Calculating discounted cash flows and finding a Profitability Index (PI) below 1.0 explicitly demonstrated why a system might be technically perfect yet business-unfeasible due to high production and maintenance costs overtaking projected returns. 
  * Furthermore, building PERT networks and tracking early/late schedules mathematically ingrained the value of finding the project's critical path—showing me exactly how to calculate slack time and minimize risk when resource allocation becomes tight.

* **Focus Areas**: Process Requirements, Context Diagrams (Level-0), Level-0 Explosion Diagrams, Child Diagram Decomposition, Data Stores, and Logical Information Flow.
* **Reflective Insight**: 
  * Translating descriptive business narratives into multi-tier logical data flows shifted my mindset from code-first thinking to structured systems thinking. Working on complex processes (such as printing management systems and meal delivery tracking) taught me the strict discipline of balanced input/output vectors.
  * I discovered that diving deep into Level-0 diagram decomposition down to detailed Child processes (e.g., separating payment collection, compilation, and automated performance reporting variables) prevents hidden scope creep before any layout or backend development begins. DFDs gave me a universal visual language to systematically identify architectural requirements and bottlenecks.

### 🚀 Final Group Project: End-to-End Online System for KADA Cooperative

* **Focus Areas**: Lembaga Kemajuan Pertanian Kemubu (KADA) manual process upgrade, logical/physical DFDs (AS-IS vs. TO-BE models), CRUD Matrices, Event-Response tables, Structural Charts, Multi-Tier System Architecture, and Interactive Figma High-Fidelity Prototyping.
* **Reflective Insight**: 
  * This extensive, collaborative project synthesized every single principle taught throughout the course into a tangible software architecture design. Partnering with the KADA cooperative to digitize their legacy, manual paper-based registration and loan application systems showed me how information systems directly drive real-world operational efficiency.
  * Gathering requirements through structured online interviews with KADA representatives and converting those needs into formal TO-BE Logical and Physical DFDs was an amazing experience. Mapping out the 9 distinct system components (ranging from registration and validation, to automated profit calculations at a fixed rate of 4.20%, to system backup scripts) highlighted the importance of clean, reliable data routing. 
  * Using advanced structural techniques like CRUD matrices, event-response maps, and multi-tier system architectures (Client $\rightarrow$ Security $\rightarrow$ Presentation $\rightarrow$ Business $\rightarrow$ Database Layer) ensured our layout could safely manage up to 2,000 parallel active sessions without operational bottlenecking.
  * Translating our technical diagrams into a comprehensive, high-fidelity interactive prototype inside Figma gave us instant confirmation that our platform met our client's exact operational goals. Ultimately, this journey proved that successful system design requires an intersection of data consistency, strong security configurations, critical problem-solving, and strict professional collaboration.
