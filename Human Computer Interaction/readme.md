## Assignment 1 — Persuasive Technology Analysis (Lazada)

Analysing Lazada through the Fogg Behaviour Model made me realise how deliberately everyday app features are designed to nudge user behaviour. Breaking down the payment options and notification system into Ability, Motivation, and Trigger showed me that a checkout page or a push notification is never just functional — it is persuasive by design. I had never consciously thought about an installment plan as a facilitator trigger before this assignment.

## Assignment 2 — Heuristic Evaluation (HealthMax)

Evaluating another group's prototype using Nielsen's heuristics shifted my thinking from *"does this look good?"* to *"does this work correctly for the user?"* The most impactful finding was the missing appointment availability indicator — a catastrophic issue not because anything crashed, but because a missing piece of information could cause a real-world error. The User Journey Map reinforced this by making usability problems feel human rather than abstract; tracing a sick user's frustration through a confusing booking flow made the stakes clear.

## Overall

HCI taught me to evaluate interfaces from a user's perspective rather than a developer's. That shift in lens is something I will carry into every design decision going forward.
